Introduction to digital accessibility
Accessibility is the degree to which
all people can perceive, understand and navigate
the information contained in a digital document
regardless of their technical, cognitive or physical abilities.
Accessibility statement
The University of Alicante is committed to making its websites and applications for mobile devices accessible, according to the
Real Decree 1112/2018
, 7th September, about accessibility of websites and mobile applications of public sector.
This accessibility statement applies to websites in the domain
www.ua.es
or the equivalent
web.ua.es
.
You can also check the
mobile applications accessibility statement of the University of Alicante
.
Compliance status
This website is partially compliant with level AA of the
Web Content Accessibility Guidelines
in version 2.1, due to the exceptions and lack of conformity in the following aspects.
Non-accessible content
The content below is not accessible for the following reasons:
Because a content management system is used and there are hundreds of users updating and creating new content, we cannot guarantee that the content will comply with the WCAG 2.1. However, we undertake to review the content periodically and to resolve any problems that users notify us of.
There is video, audio or video and audio old multimedia contents that do not comply with the AA level of accessibility because they do not have subtitles or text transcription.
There is live multimedia content.
You can find office files in PDF, Word, PowerPoint and other formats, as well as applications published before September 20, 2018, which do not meet all accessibility requirements.
Online map and cartography services.
Third party content not financed or developed by the University of Alicante.
Preparation of this accessibility statement
This statement was prepared on 25 October 2019 after review of the home page by the University's technical staff and by users with various disabilities.
Observations and contact details
If you have any queries, complaints or suggestions regarding the accessibility of the University of Alicante's websites and applications for mobile devices, or if you wish to request information in an accessible format, you can send them to us.
Form for queries, complaints and suggestions
If you need information about how to use this website with support products or technical aids, you can write to us at
accesibilidad@ua.es
.
Application procedure
Once a request for accessible information or complaint has been made, if it has been rejected, there is no agreement on the decision taken or if the response does not satisfy the requirements referred to in Article 12.5, the interested person may initiate a claim. Likewise, a claim may be initiated if the period of twenty business days has elapsed without having obtained an answer.
The claim can be submitted through a generic instance in the General Registry of the University of Alicante or an instance from the Sede Electronica, as well as the rest of the options included in Law 39/2015, of October 1, of the Common Administrative Procedure of the Public Administrations.
Claims will be received and treated by the Responsible Unit for Accessibility of the University of Alicante.

Text size
The accessible design of this web allows the users to choose the font size they need. This action can be done by different ways depending on the browser.
One way to modify the text size on Windows computers is to use the key combination CTRL and + to increase the text size, and CTRL and - to reduce the size of the text.
You can also follow the next steps depending on the browser you use to change the text size.

Page Name: 19-25 url: https://web.ua.es/en/actualidad-universitaria/2024/february2024/19-25/university-of-alicante-obtains-an-enia-research-chair-for-the-development-of-ethical-and-responsible-artificial-intelligence.html | Content:
University of Alicante obtains an ENIA Research Chair for the development of ethical and responsible Artificial Intelligence
The University of Alicante ENIA research chair will carry out research to guarantee the ethics and fairness of decision-making based on the implementation of AI in the private and public sector
Alicante,  Thursday 22 February 2024
The University of Alicante has been one of those selected by the Ministry of Economic Affairs and Digital Transformation to create university research chairs dedicated to dissemination, teaching and innovation on Artificial Intelligence. In particular, the UA project chosen is a holistic approach for the development of ethical and responsible Artificial Intelligence.
The line of work for the University of Alicante Enia Research Chair will focus on ensuring decision-making based on fair and ethical usage of AI.
One aspect of AI development has been a constant concern of the European Union, which has emerged as the world's leading guarantor of citizens' rights in terms of protection, use (including by AI), re-use and rights over citizen-generated data. Along these lines, in April 2021, the European Commission approved the European Union's first regulatory framework for Artificial Intelligence. This places special emphasis on critical aspects surrounding AI, such as guaranteeing fair and ethical decisions to be adopted or enacting the need for the explainability of models, especially in critical sectors such as health or autonomous driving, among others, given its high impact on the citizens' quality of life.
The advance in the application of AI makes it increasingly difficult to guarantee these aspects in the development of AI-based applications as they learn from huge amounts of Big Data sources. An example of this are applications based on Large Language Models (LLMs), such as ChatGPT, where the millions of variables it handles make it practically unfeasible to detect the ethical aspects mentioned above.
The University of Alicante ENIA Research Chair will be directed by Professor Juan Carlos Trujillo, an expert in processing and analysis of Big Data and Artificial Intelligence. Trujillo is among the most cited researchers (H index) and recognised worldwide in his main research topics, as well as having a long experience in the management of competitive research projects and knowledge transfer. Juan C. Trujillo has specialised in the detection of biases in training data, the application of AI in health and is currently addressing the explainability of LLMs.
The UA researcher stresses that Big Data is the fuel of AI. One of the reasons for the boom in AI and its impact on practically all economic sectors is the constant generation of huge amounts of data and the processing and analysis capacity of Big Data. The more data, the more Machine Learning and Deep Learning models will be able to learn and the better decisions will be made.
UA Enia Research Chair
The UA ENIA Research Chair has a marked interdisciplinary and multidisciplinary nature, since research from several fields—other than AI developers—are part of the team. It is noteworthy that half of the members from the discipline of Law, providing world-class AI experts in ethics and civil liability. According to the UA ENIA Research Chair Director, this makes therm optimistic about the research to be carried out, which will take the UA to the forefront of the development of AI applications that guarantee their ethics and responsibility. He also pointed out that another aspect to be highlighted is a gender-balanced workforce, which is 50%.
Some of the lines of work that will be addressed in the project are data biases, the explainability and reliability of AI, the hybridisation of AI and Big Data, the legal regime of open data and Big Data, and civil liability and insurance associated with AI.
The ENIA Research Chair has the advice and participation of PhD Nuria Oliver. In addition,  the ELLIS Alicante node is also involved in the project, directed by Oliver herself and committed to a human-centered approach to AI ethics. Thus, the advances made within the Research Chair will be disseminated in the European ELLIS Network. Additionally, several participants are also collaborating with the CLAIRE Network, which guarantees maximum international spread of the results obtained. In addition to these two networks, other national and international dissemination activities have been designed to maximise the impact. This way, the UA ENIA Research Chair brings together this broadly multidisciplinary and highly talented group, which is expected to make significant advances in the coming years.
Knowledge transfer
A noteworthy aspect of the UA ENIA Research Chair is the support and direct participation of some top technology-based companies. Within this project, the development of Artificial Intelligence solutions has been planned so that these companies continue to be at the forefront of technology in their respective sectors.
Worth mentioning are sponsoring companies such as AVAMED, Bumerania, StrateBI, and OneMillionBot, with which innovation problems have been jointly defined so that the researchprogramme can bring them a quick return and added value compared to their competitors. Other sponsoring companies, such as Balearia and Lucentia Lab, will actively collaborate to incorporate the main technological solutions derived from this Research Chair into their business models.
In addition, the proposal of the UA ENIA Research Chair also highlights the strong innovative and entrepreneurial nature of the city of Alicante, where initiatives such as the UA Science Park, Distrito Digital (Valencian Government), or Alicante Futura (Alicante City Council), with dozens of technology companies. This allows for an innovative ecosystem in the city of Alicante, in which the University of Alicante is a benchmark through this project.
The UA ENIA Research Chair has received great institutional support from organisations of  a variety of ideological and political signs, including the city councils of Alicante and San Vicente, the Alicante Provincial Council, the Valencian Government, the Chamber of Commerce, AEPA, ICOPAL, ICALUI, CEV or UEPAL.
En la imagen, de izquierda a derecha, algunos de los integrantes de la Cátedra Enia de la UA: José García, Jorge Calvo y Juan Carlos Trujillo, profesores de la Escuela Politécnica Superior y Aurora Ribes, Cristina Berenguer, Julián López, Begoña Pérez y Yolanda Bustos de la Facultad de Derecho de la UA.

Structure of material ejected from asteroid Dimorphos after DART spacecraft collision comes to light
An international paper published in
Nature
provides a new snapshot of what happened in the seconds after the impact
The paper, led by the National Institute for Astrophysics in Italy, is co-authored by Adriano Campo Bagatin, professor at the University of Alicante
LICIACube is a mission of the Italian Space Agency (ASI), which is part of NASA's DART mission. Credits: ASI
Alicante. Wednesday, 28 February 2024
A new article published in the prestigious journal
Nature
analyses the structure of the cloud of debris and dust (plume) ejected by asteroid Dimorphos after the impact of DART (Double Asteroid Redirection Test), the NASA planetary defence test in which the Planetary Sciences team of the Astronomy and Astrophysics Research Group of the University of Alicante (UA) is collaborating. On 26 September 2022, the DART mission directed a half-ton spacecraft fired at 22,000 km/h towards Dimorphos, the satellite of the near-Earth asteroid Didymos. That was the first planetary defence experiment ever attempted that successfully changed its trajectory.
After a year and a half under the watchful eye of the LICIACube (Light Italian Cubesat for Imaging of Asteroids) space device of the Italian Space Agency (ASI), an international team of researchers—led by the National Institute for Astrophysics- INAF (Italy) with the participation of UA Professor of Physics
Adriano Campo Bagatin
—provides another snapshot of what happened in the seconds after the impact.
In the image, the dust flows around Dimorphos (top). Below, the companion asteroid Didymos. Credit: E.Dotto/INAF/ASI
After a year and a half under the watchful eye of the LICIACube (Light Italian Cubesat for Imaging of Asteroids) space device of the Italian Space Agency (ASI), an international team of researchers—led by the National Institute for Astrophysics- INAF (Italy) with the participation of UA Professor of Physics
Adriano Campo Bagatin
—provides another snapshot of what happened in the seconds after the impact.
LICIACube, the spacecraft that accompanied the DART mission, sent back to Earth extraordinary data immediately before and after the impact and obtained 426 images of the effects produced. According to Elisabetta Dotto, INAF researcher and first author of the paper, the scientific phase began 71 seconds before DART's impact, witnessing live the measurement of a rapid change in the brightness of the small asteroid. Travelling at a speed of about 6.1 km/s (kilometres per second), LICIACube flew over the object, reaching a distance of only 58 kilometres at its closest point to Dimorphos, 174 seconds after impact.
How did Dimorphos change after the arrival of DART?
The surprising thing was that the surface of this satellite was no longer visible due to the ejected material as we had predicted from the UA in a previous study published before the arrival of DART, in collaboration with researchers from Uruguay and the Andalusian Institute for Astrophysics-IAA in Granada (Spain). Additionally, to witnessing the unique event of the deflection of an asteroid due to a kinetic impact, detailed images of the effects of the collision have been obtained. In fact, UA professor explained that they have been able to better understand how these small asteroids react to collisions and, from this, to deduce information about the nature of these objects.
Also, Campo Bagatin stated that this mission—as part of the asteroid impact risk mitigation strategy. DART and LICIACube, together with the European Space Agency's Hera space mission, which will be launched in October this year—will provide a complete picture of the physical characteristics and structure of the bodies that make up this binary asteroid. This allows researchers to interpret the results of the collision on Dimorphos in a timely manner. Moreover, since asteroids are what remains of an intermediate stage in the process that led to the formation of the planets, the data acquired provide important information in the study of the early stages of aggregation of the material that makes up the Solar Systems.
As detailed in the paper published in
Nature,
the material ejected from the impact crater formed a cone with an opening angle of about 140 degrees and a complex and heterogeneous structure, characterised by filaments, dust grains and individual or clustered fragments, even as large as a few metres, ejected as a result of DART's impact. The images show that the innermost part of the cone had a bluish colour and gradually became redder as the distance to Dimorphos increased. The velocity of the ejected material ranged from a few tens of m/s (metre per second) to about 500 m/s, although there is evidence that a lot of material was also ejected at a few tens of cm/s.
The results obtained by the Italian mission are scientifically relevant for the international community, as they are the only images collected in situ from the first planetary defence mission to date. As reported by Campo Bagatin, they are currently collaborating on the physical interpretation of some features shown by the images that have not yet been satisfactorily explained.

Page Name: 19-25 url: https://web.ua.es/en/actualidad-universitaria/2024/february2024/19-25/ua-leads-a-european-project-to-improve-drinking-water-pollution.html | Content:
UA leads a European project to improve drinking water pollution
Drinking water contamination is a major health concern in rural areas in some of the project partner countries such as Ukraine, Mexico, Moldova and Kazakhstan
Alicante, Monday 19 February 2024
The
Advanced Materials Laboratory
(LMA) of the University of Alicante (UA) leads the European project Cleanwater
(Multifunctional sustainable adsorbents for water treatment assisted with plasma technologies and for health protection from xenobiotics)
with the aim of developing adsorbent materials for application in portable water treatment systems.
Drinking water contamination is a global health concern—mainly in rural areas—with larger number of vulnerable populations every time. This challenge requires a unique, easy-to-handle, low-cost solution capable of reducing the levels of chemical and radiological pathogens in a single, simple container.
With a duration of four years—until the end of 2027—and the collaboration of thirteen partners from universities, institutes and technology centres and companies from Budapest, the UK, Poland, Moldova, Kazakhstan, Ukraine and Mexico, Cleanwater aims to generate a novel water treatment technology that is inexpensive and easy to install through the application of plasma in the presence of adsorbent beds. The combination of these processes in a single device will improve the efficiency of water decontamination processes for human consumption. In other words, it will be possible to eliminate chemical substances such as xenobiotics, emerging pollutants and persistent pollutants, among others, as explained by project's principal researcher and UA professor
Joaquín Silvestre
.
Additionally, the project team intends to use these adsorbent materials—previously modified—for their application in the body for the same purpose: the elimination of external pollutants ingested orally or through the skin, as explained by Silvestre.
Induced climate change, natural disasters and wars such as the one in Ukraine have made the search for quick and effective solutions to prevent the spread of waterborne epidemics and exposure to unsafe levels of heavy metals or dangerous pollutants particularly relevant. The complexity of such contamination with different organic and inorganic components requires a multi-component system or device, in tablet form, capable of specifically addressing each of these hazards at the same time.
In this context, according to the UA researcher, the project will have a very positive impact on rural communities where there is no access to safe drinking water, which is the case for some countries participating in the project, such as Ukraine, Mexico, Moldova and Kazakhstan. It should be noted that the contamination of natural water has a very negative impact on women, especially pregnant women, children and the elderly. Therefore, these are the groups that will benefit most from the project.
The Advanced Materials Laboratory of the UA Department of Inorganic Chemistry has a long history in nanomaterials capable of adsorbing toxic substances, in fact, this group is a world leader in the synthesis and application of porous materials for pollutant removal processes, both in gas and liquid phase. This experience is a key element in the development of the European Cleanwater project, funded with around €980,000.

Page Name: 11-17 url: https://web.ua.es/es/actualidad-universitaria/2024/marzo2024/11-17/la-ua-lidera-un-proyecto-para-mejorar-la-rehabilitacion-de-pacientes-con-trastornos-neuromusculares.html | Content:
La UA lidera un proyecto para mejorar la rehabilitación de pacientes con trastornos neuromusculares
El objetivo final es desarrollar un sistema basado en IA para monitorizar y ofrecer una intervención personalizada de rehabilitación de la mano
El consorcio, formado instituciones de España, Alemania, Brasil y Panamá, trabajará con pacientes que han sufrido un ictus o una lesión medular
Pruebas en laboratorio de la UA con los miembros de HURO Cristina Romero, Andrés Úbeda y Lluís Bernat. Foto de equipo: Lluís Bernat (izq.), Andrés Úbeda (centro) y Cristina Romero (dcha.).             Autor: Rafa Pastor (UA).
Alicante. Miércoles, 13 de marzo de 2024
El grupo de investigación Human Robotics (
HURO
) de la Universidad de Alicante (UA) lidera el proyecto
Myorehab
(“Monitoring and delivering personalised hand  neurorehabilitation through virtual activities controlled by the neural drive”) para mejorar la rehabilitación de pacientes con trastornos neuromusculares. En concreto, un equipo internacional está trabajando en un modelo neuromecánico personalizado para personas con afecciones motoras en la mano tras sufrir un ictus o una lesión medular.
Tal y como explica el investigador de HURO y coordinador del proyecto, Andrés Úbeda, «el objetivo de Myorehab es proporcionar un sistema de inteligencia artificial (IA) que pueda monitorizar y ofrecer una intervención personalizada de rehabilitación de la mano basada en la cantidad de actividad eléctrica generada por los músculos». De forma complementaria a la terapia convencional, el proyecto implementa tecnologías punteras como electromiografía de alta densidad, y actividades virtuales gamificadas para mejorar y monitorizar la rehabilitación en un gran número de pacientes.
Gracias a este nuevo modelo se van a poder extraer biomarcadores neuromecánicos asociados a la rehabilitación motora y se podrá evaluar la intervención de gamificación basada en IA para ofrecer una rehabilitación totalmente adaptada a cada paciente.
Los tratamientos de rehabilitación de los trastornos neuromusculares varían según los centros clínicos y los sistemas sanitarios «por lo que hace falta un modelo estandarizado que se puede replicar en cualquier país», añade Úbeda. Una de las principales dificultades del tratamiento es poder hacer una evaluación cuantitativa de como el paciente mejora. Es en este punto donde el proyecto Myorehab ayudará tanto al profesional rehabilitador como al paciente a valorar el proceso completo desde el inicio de las sesiones.
En una primera fase, la tecnología y los protocolos de rehabilitación se aplicarán y probarán en individuos sanos para, después, llevar a cabo una evaluación clínica. En esta segunda fase, el nuevo modelo se utilizará para extraer métricas clínicas útiles para readaptar las terapias físicas de forma más eficiente y eficaz. «La meta de este proyecto es llegar a la fase de intervención clínica donde la terapia convencional se complementará con esta novedosa tecnología para mejorar la recuperación proporcionando a los pacientes actividades de rehabilitación más atractivas y eficaces. En el caso de Alicante, desde la UA colaboraremos con pacientes afectados por ictus del Hospital General de Alicante», apunta Úbeda.
Con una duración de tres años, hasta mediados del 2026, Myorehab reúne a instituciones de España, Alemania, Brasil y Panamá. Una sólida red de investigación a largo plazo entre investigadores europeos y latinoamericanos para proporcionar un marco transnacional en el área de la rehabilitación motora estandarizada. Participan, además de la UA, la Universidad de Erlangen-Núremberg (FAU) de Alemania, la Universidad Estatal de Campinas (UNICAMP) y la Universidad Federal de Pernambuco (UFPE), ambas de Brasil, y la Universidad Tecnológica de Panamá (UTP).
El proyecto Myorehab cuenta con una financiación de más de 485.000 euros financiados a través de la 4ª convocatoria multitemática para proyectos de investigación e innovación 2022 del Grupo de Interés
EU-LAC
formado por agencias financiadoras de América Latina, Caribe y Europa. En el caso de España, la financiación parte del Ministerio de Ciencia, Innovación y Universidades.
HURO
El grupo de investigación de Human Robotics (HURO), perteneciente al Departamento de Física, Ingeniería de Sistemas y Teoría de la Señal de la Universidad de Alicante, se creó en 2018 para dar respuesta a los nuevos desafíos relacionados con el mundo de la robótica, un sector que requiere robots capaces de desarrollar sus tareas en entornos dinámicos y sociales. Sus miembros, con gran experiencia en robótica y sistemas automatizados, trabajan principalmente en cinco líneas de investigación: la robótica de rehabilitación, la neuromecánica, la robótica espacial, la visión y la inteligencia artificial y la realidad virtual y aumentada.
Consorcio internacional (España, Alemania, Brasil y Panamá) del proyecto Myorehab. Cedida por los investigadores.

Filosofía y Letras y la Escuela Politécnica Superior consiguen acreditaciones institucionales de calidad de ANECA
Estas certificaciones como entidad en conjunto otorgan a ambos centros de la UA la capacidad de reacreaditar todos sus títulos formativos
Desde ahora, ambas entidades han de acreditar su calidad institucional a la agencia nacional cada seis años en lugar de hacerlo individualmente para cada título
Imágenes de archivo de la Facultad de Filosofía y Letras y de la EPS.
Alicante. Jueves, 14 de marzo de 2024
La
Facultad de Filosofía y Letras
y la
Escuela Politécnica Superior (EPS)
de la
Universidad de Alicante (UA)
han conseguido sendas acreditaciones institucionales de la Agencia Nacional de Evaluación de la Calidad y Acreditación (Aneca) como centros de calidad en su conjunto, hecho que supone un paso adelante en la reacreditación de títulos ya que, previamente a este logro, cada centro debía reacreditar la calidad de cada uno de sus títulos por separado. Una vez el Consejo de Universidades aprobó el pasado lunes estos certificados, los centros han visto acreditados todos sus títulos.
La concesión del certificado institucional de calidad a un centro es un importante logro para acreditar su calidad, ya que garantiza que todos los títulos que imparte han superado los estándares pertinentes para ello. Según explica Francisco Torres, vicerrector de
Estudios, Calidad y Lenguas de la UA
, «conseguir estas acreditaciones en dos centros culmina varios años de trabajo y muchas certificaciones, con sus informes anuales de seguimiento de los títulos formativos», a lo que añade que «tener una acreditación institucional por la Aneca significa que el centro tiene un sistema de garantía interno de calidad, robusto y que cumple los más altos estándares de rigor, compromiso y transparencia».
Además del reconocimiento en sí que suponen estas acreditaciones, también posibilitan efectos prácticos como la agilización de trámites de gestión administrativa con Aneca (algunos trámites bajan de seis a cuatro meses) y la reducción de documentación a presentar en los procesos de modificación de títulos. También permite considerar reacreditados todos los títulos del centro, tanto los títulos de grado como los másteres, que ya no tienen que pasar procesos individuales de reacreditación, sino que es el centro el que se reacredita institucionalmente cada seis años.
La evaluación de la solicitud de acreditación institucional se lleva a cabo por el Comité Asesor para la Acreditación Institucional Inicial de ANECA e incluye la comprobación del estado de los títulos del centro en el Registro de Universidades, Centros y Títulos (RUCT), así como la comprobación de la vigencia del certificado de implantación del Sistema Interno de Garantía de Calidad (SIGC). Desde cada centro, con el apoyo del Vicerrectorado de Estudios, Calidad y Lenguas y la Unidad Técnica de Calidad de la UA, se envió a Aneca el pertinente informe. Una vez dado resultado favorable a los documentos por la agencia nacional, fueron enviados al Consejo de Universidades para que culminase su tramitación y aprobación en la reunión que tuvo lugar el pasado lunes.
Ya aprobadas estas certificaciones institucionales, Filosofía y Letras y la EPS son los primeros centros en obtener este certificado institucional en la Universidad de Alicante, pero «confiamos en que no serán los únicos, porque todos los centros han presentado los informes para este paso y es cuestión de tiempo que más facultades la obtengan», detalla Torres, a lo que añade que «la intención del Vicerrectorado de Estudios, Calidad y Lenguas es que todos los centros de la UA estén acreditados institucionalmente».
En el mismo sentido, cabe señalar que, de todas las universidades de la Comunitat Valenciana, únicamente la Universitat Jaume I de Castellón tiene algún centro acreditado. En concreto, esta institución consiguió la acreditación para los tres centros de la componen. Ahora, con estas acreditaciones, la UA es la segunda institución académica de la Comunitat Valenciana que logra este tipo de acreditación en alguna de sus facultades.

Page Name: 11-17 url: https://web.ua.es/es/actualidad-universitaria/2024/marzo2024/11-17/el-investigador-de-la-ua-javier-garcia-recoge-el-premio-nacional-de-investigacion-juan-de-la-cierva.html | Content:
El investigador de la UA Javier García recoge el Premio Nacional de Investigación “Juan de la Cierva”
La ceremonia de entrega de los Premios Nacionales de Investigación se ha celebrado en el Palacio Ducal de los Borja en Gandía presidida por los Reyes de España
García ha reivindicado “la certidumbre de la ciencia” ante los problemas que debe enfrentar la sociedad
Gandia. Jueves, 14 de marzo de 2024
El catedrático de
Química Inorgánica
de la Universidad de Alicante, Javier García Martínez, ha recibido hoy, de manos de los Reyes de España, el Premio Nacional de Investigación ‘Juan de la Cierva’, en el área de Transferencia de Tecnología en una ceremonia que ha tenido lugar en el Palacio Ducal de los Borja en Gandía.
El acto se ha celebrado a las 12 horas conducido por la periodista Lara Siscar y, tras unas palabras de la ministra de Ciencia, Innovación y Universidades, Diana Morant, se han proyectado los vídeos de los premiados y los Reyes han hecho entrega de los galardones. La ceremonia ha concluido con una mesa redonda en la que han intervenido representantes de los Premios Nacionales de Investigación 2023 que hoy se han entregado, entre los que se encontraba el catedrático de la UA, Javier García.
Javier García, investigador de la Universidad de Alicante ha señalado que “recibir el Premio Nacional de Investigación supone sobre todo un reconocimiento muy importante al trabajo que venimos realizando en el Laboratorio de Nanotecnología Molecular de la UA. Aquí hemos desarrollado nuevos procesos químicos que nos permiten desarrollar y transformar residuos en productos de alto valor añadido, convertir plásticos en combustibles limpios y fabricar medicamentos de una forma mucho más sostenible”.
García ha recalcado que la investigación que le ha merecido la alta distinción “ha servido para desarrollar  y transferir al mercado una nueva familia de catalizadores que se utilizan hoy en todo el mundo para reducir las emisiones de CO2 y que están comercializando diferentes multinacionales”.
El investigador de la UA también ha asegurado que “estos Premios Nacionales de Investigación son muy relevantes ya que es clave reconocer la mejor investigación que se hace en nuestro país. Y es que es posible hacer investigación de vanguardia en España. Este mensaje es clave porqué, sin duda, ayudará a crear nuevas vocaciones científicas en nuestro país. La ciencia no es algo accesorio, no es un lujo, es algo prioritario, está en la base de las nuevas empresas que están redefiniendo nuestro futuro, bien sean las que están basadas en IA, en nanotecnología o en edición genética”.
Por último, el nuevo Premio Nacional de Investigación ha apostillado que “los problemas a los que nos enfrentamos sólo podrán ser superados con más y mejor ciencia. En un tiempo caracterizado por la incertidumbre, económica, social y geopolítica, necesitamos la certidumbre y las soluciones que nos aporta la ciencia y tomar decisiones basadas en evidencias”.
A la ceremonia de entrega de los premios también ha asistido el vicerrector de
Investigación
de la Universidad de Alicante, Juan Mora, quién ha querido “ expresar mi más sincera enhorabuena al profesor García Martínez por este premio fruto de su indudable talento y esfuerzo investigador que le ha llevado a convertirse en un referente internacional en el ámbito de la química de los nanomateriales”.
Mora ha asegurado que “para la Universidad de Alicante es un orgullo contar con un nuevo investigador galardonado con el Premio Nacional de Investigación, el más alto reconocimiento a la ciencia otorgado por el Gobierno de España. El Premio, segundo consecutivo recibido por un investigador de la Universidad de Alicante, es un reconocimiento a la excelencia de la actividad investigadora llevada a cabo en nuestro campus y supone un impulso a la investigación y a la transferencia de conocimiento en nuestra universidad”.
Por su parte, el Rey, encargado de cerrar el acto, ha reivindicado el papel de la ciencia y de los científicos para “el fortalecimiento de nuestro país a través de su excelente labor” y ha recalcado que todo “su trabajo y dedicación merecen nuestra gratitud y estima”. Felipe VI ha señalado que las “personas que hoy reconocemos son necesarias y son un orgullo para un país que tiene ambición de futuro. Un futuro que está íntimamente ligado a la investigación científica para lograr el mejor futuro posible y llegar a las generaciones venideras”.
Su majestad ha finalizado su intervención parafraseando a Vicent Andrés Estellés “la ciencia es la llave que abre todas las puertas”, ha afirmado y ha recordado que “es necesario reiterar que invertir en ciencia es invertir en futuro”.
La ministra de de Ciencia, Innovación y Universidades, Diana Morant, se ha referido en su intervención a los premiados como “los arquitectos de nuestro futuro” y ha reseñado que “fortalecer la investigación es la clave para fortalecer a toda la ciudadanía”.

AntiFraude
El Buzón de Denuncias es un canal electrónico seguro que permite a la comunidad universitaria presentar denuncias dentro del marco del Plan Antifraude.
La Universidad de Alicante, comprometida con una política de tolerancia cero con la corrupción y el fraude, aprobó en su Consejo de Gobierno de fecha 20 de diciembre de 2021, una declaración institucional en la cual hacía público su compromiso con las medidas establecidas por la Unión Europea contra el fraude y la corrupción.
Este buzón incorpora mecanismos que garantizan la confidencialidad de todas las informaciones y ofrece un espacio de comunicación seguro para mantener contacto con la Comisión Antifraude de la Universidad de Alicante y permite la interacción entre ésta y los denunciantes de manera confidencial. El anonimato en las comunicaciones también se asegura siempre que el entorno digital en el que se realice la comunicación así lo garantice
¿Ha detectado algún tipo de fraude en una administración en el ámbito de la Comunidad Valenciana y puede aportar pruebas de esta conducta?
Utilizando la aplicación, que garantiza la confidencialidad, puede hacer que se investigue el caso y se actúe para mejorar la integridad del sector público.
Puede dar sus datos o mantener una comunicación completamente anónima. En cualquier caso podemos protegerle por medio del estatuto del denunciante.
Puede hacer el seguimiento de la comunicación a través de un canal seguro y conversar con el órgano gestor del buzón, independiente en el ejercicio de sus funciones.
Acceder al Canal Antifraude
¿Qué es y para qué sirve el Buzón de Denuncias de la Universidad de Alicante?
El Canal Antifraude de la Universidad de Alicante
constituye un instrumento de comunicación
al alcance de las personas que integran la comunidad universitaria (personal docente e investigador, el personal de administración y servicios y el colectivo de estudiantes) y demás personas que tengan relación directa o indirecta con ella (proveedores, prestadores de servicios externos, estudiantes en prácticas, etc.),
para facilitar, de forma identificada o anónima, cualquier información relativa a conductas contrarias a la legalidad y a los principios o reglas éticas y de buen gobierno
, así como aquellos hechos que puedan constituir una infracción penal o implicar la materialización de un riesgo de tal naturaleza.
Las comunicaciones realizadas a través del Canal Antifraude de la Universidad de Alicantese deben referir a hechos, acciones u omisiones
dentro del ámbito de actuación de la Universidad de Alicante
.
Objetivos del Buzón
Facilitar la presentación de denuncias.
Garantizar la confidencialidad del denunciante.
Servir de herramienta para que se puedan cumplir varias de las funciones principales de la Agencia Valenciana Antifraude:
La investigación de posibles casos de uso o destino irregular de fondos públicos y de conductas opuestas a la integridad o contrarias a los principios de objetividad, eficacia y sumisión plena a la ley y al derecho.
La alerta con relación a conductas del personal al servicio de las entidades públicas que comporten el uso o abuso en beneficio privado de informaciones que tengan por razón de sus funciones o que tengan o puedan tener como resultado el uso o el destino irregular de fondos públicos o de cualquier otro aprovechamiento contrario al ordenamiento jurídico.
La investigación de los actos o las omisiones que pudieran ser constitutivos de infracción administrativa, disciplinaria o penal y, en función de los resultados de la investigación, instar la incoación de los procedimientos que corresponda para depurar las responsabilidades que pudieran corresponder.
Ámbito de actuación
Las comunicaciones se tienen que referir
a acciones u omisiones que se produzcan en el ámbito de la Universidad de Alicante por la actuación de
:
Cualquiera persona miembro de la comunidad universitaria
: del personal docente e investigador, del personal de administración y servicios y del colectivo de estudiantes.
Instituciones o empresas que contraten o que colaboren con la Universidad de Alicante
, cuando así lo disponga el contrato o convenio correspondiente o lo aprueben sus órganos de gobierno, en sus relaciones con la Universidad y con las personas, en el marco de la ejecución del contrato correspondiente.
Las personas y las entidades que reciban subvenciones de la Universidad
, cuando así lo disponga el convenio o las bases reguladoras, en el marco de la actuación subvencionada por la Universidad…
¿Sobre qué puedo informar?
A modo de ejemplo, usted puede informar de las siguientes conductas:
Uso o destino ilegal de fondos públicos.
Desvío de subvenciones a fines ajenos a aquellos para los que se han otorgado.
Irregularidades en la contratación pública.
Arbitrariedades que determinan un acceso desigual a bienes, subvenciones y servicios públicos.
Aprovechamiento particular y arbitrario de bienes e instalaciones públicas.
Conflictos de interés, incumplimientos del deber de abstención y de la normativa de incompatibilidades.
Aceptación de regalos o de tratos de favor por parte de servidores públicos.
Uso en beneficio privado de informaciones obtenidas en el ejercicio de funciones públicas.
Lucro patrimonial injustificado de autoridades y funcionarios, derivado del ejercicio de sus responsabilidades públicas.
Falta de transparencia y cualquier forma de obstaculización en el acceso a la información pública.
Este Canal, no puede actuar ante fraudes de naturaleza privada o que no estén en el ámbito de actuación de la Universidad.
Requisitos
Para poder dar tratamiento a la comunicación, esta deberá contener unos elementos mínimos que lleven al inicio del correspondiente procedimiento por parte del órgano encargado del control, gestión y seguimiento de las comunicaciones recibidas.
Por tanto, los requisitos mínimos que deberá contener serán los siguientes
:
Identificación del informante, que deberá ser tratada con la debida confidencialidad
, y sobre la que, en todo caso, habrá que cumplir con la normativa aplicable en materia de protección de informantes y de protección de datos de carácter personal. No obstante,
se podrán recibir comunicaciones anónimas,
estando obligado el Órgano de control, gestión y seguimiento a iniciar el expediente correspondiente.
Descripción detallada del hecho o situación sospechosa
, indicando, en la medida de lo posible:
En qué consiste la conducta o situación potencial o actualmente irregular.
Posibles personas implicadas.
Fechas aproximadas del suceso.
Medios a través de los que se ha realizado la posible conducta ilícita.
Área de actividad afectada.
Posible impacto económico.
Otros posibles impactos en la UA.
Si fuese posible, se aportarán documentos o evidencias de los hechos.
¿Cómo funciona?
Una vez presentada una comunicación por medio del Canal,
los informantes obtienen de la aplicación un acuse de recibo en el que se les confirma que la comunicación ha sido realizada con éxito
. Este acuse de recibo
incorpora un código alfanumérico de acceso a un canal de comunicación confidencial
.
El código asignado cuando se accede al Canal es la única forma de acceso y es responsabilidad del informante conservarlo
. Este canal seguro de comunicación electrónica garantiza la confidencialidad de las comunicaciones y ofrece la posibilidad de preservar el anonimato. Permite además mantener la colaboración con la el Órgano gestor del Buzón en la comprobación de los hechos.
Es importante que se mantenga el diálogo entre los informantes y el Órgano gestor
por medio de este canal para facilitar las tareas de investigación y comprobar los hechos.
La falta de respuesta a los requerimientos de información formulados a través del Canal Antifraude puede suponer el archivo de las actuaciones.
Seguimiento
Una vez efectuada la comunicación,
el Órgano gestor acusará recibo de la misma al informante,
en el caso de que se identifique, en el plazo de siete días naturales
a partir del día siguiente a la recepción.
Si la comunicación no reflejara los datos con suficiente claridad
,
se requerirá al informante
a través del canal o del medio en el que se haya recibido la comunicación,
para que subsane los defectos advertidos en el plazo de diez días hábiles
, con observación de que, si no lo hiciera, se tendrá la comunicación por no admitida a trámite.
Las actuaciones se extenderán por un periodo máximo de tres meses desde el acuse de recibo, que podría ampliarse a seis cuando sea necesario debido a circunstancias específicas del caso
, en particular la naturaleza y la complejidad del objeto de los hechos informados, que puedan justificar esta ampliación.
Al finalizar
esta tramitación y verificación de hechos, se emitirá  un informe de valoración, con las conclusiones y propuestas que se deriven de la misma, que se elevará al Rector, donde
se formularán recomendaciones con medidas de mejora en la gestión pública
, o se propondrá al órgano competente iniciar expedientes administrativos para restaurar la legalidad alterada, la adopción de medidas sancionadoras o disciplinarias o la comunicación a la fiscalía de las conductas que puedan resultar constitutivas de ilícito penal.
Durante todo el proceso
se adoptarán mecanismos para garantizar
, tanto
la integridad
de las personas que aporten información, como la de aquellas que se vean afectadas por los hechos o situaciones a los que haga referencia la misma.
Comunicaciones anónimas
El Canal garantiza, en todo momento, la confidencialidad de las comunicaciones y la indemnidad del comunicador.
Se pueden facilitar los datos identificativos y de contacto o, si se prefiere, también puede hacerse la comunicación de forma anónima.
Tiene dos opciones para hacer la comunicación de forma anónima:
Utilizando su navegador, pero
sin facilitar los datos identificativos y de contacto
. En este caso, queda rastro de la dirección IP desde la que se hace la comunicación.
Garantizando totalmente el anonimato
de la comunicación en el entorno digital (también de la dirección IP, que puede identificar a quien navega por internet), utilizando
una red de anonimización. La herramienta más utilizada es la red TOR.
Como cualquier otro navegador, para hacer uso de la herramienta TOR, hay que descargar el navegador desde la página
https://www.torproject.org/download/download-easy.html.es.
Video tutorial sobre cómo descargar el TOR
.
El análisis de las herramientas usadas por otras entidades dedicadas a la denuncia ciudadana y expertos en estos temas,
aconsejan facilitar esta opción de anonimato para preservar la integridad de quien informa
, que a menudo puede estar en situación de vulnerabilidad.
La red TOR es básicamente un software gratuito y de código abierto que permite mejorar la privacidad y seguridad en internet
. Al conectarse a internet con TOR, la conexión pasa a través de una serie de túneles cifrados, en vez de hacerlo directamente, y eso dificulta rastrear la fuente de la información. Así, la identidad de la persona que se conecta está protegida.
El uso de la red TOR para acceder al Canal es aconsejable o indispensable cuando se tiene el convencimiento o la certeza de riesgo,
porque garantiza el anonimato de la persona en el momento de la recepción de la comunicación
. No obstante, hay que recordar que quien comunica debe preservar, también, el anonimato en el momento de la emisión.
Se recuerda que los trabajadores de la Universidad y, en general, los servidores públicos que trabajan con redes institucionales no tienen acceso a esta página de descarga desde sus puestos de trabajo
. Para hacer uso del navegador TOR, deben descargárselo desde su dispositivo personal.
Se recomienda cerrar el resto de navegadores y aplicaciones mientras se hace uso de TOR.

Gender Equality
Framework for action
Visibilización y sensibilización
1.1 Develop the University of Alicante's corporate social responsibility in gender equality, organizing specific initiatives and collaborating in ones conducted by other institutions and charity organizations.
1.2 Make the university community aware of the absence of effective equality between women and men, the persistence of sexism in society and the need to contribute to the identification and the rejection of all forms of gender violence.
1.3 Make the university community -with special emphasis on students- aware of the existence of gender stereotypes related to the professional sphere, as well as to personal and family life, in order to contribute to building a society where women and men share, on equal terms and with equal opportunities, all areas of public and private life.
Docencia
2.1 Promote, disseminate and demonstrate their knowledge on gender equality
2.2 Include the gender equality approach in university education
Investigation
3.1 Promote the participation of women in both research and dissemination of knowledge.
3.2 Promote cross-curricular implementation of gender approach in research.
3.3 Promote research in gender studies from several fields of study and disseminate results.
Access and professional promotion of academic and research staff and administrative staff.
4.1 Ensure equal opportunities for women and men in selection processes and career development at the UA.
4.2 Achieve a balanced representation of women and men in the academic and administrative spheres.
Representación
5.1 Achieve a balanced representation of women and men in the UA executive officers.
5.2 Achieve a balanced representation of women and men in all the UA governing, advisory and representation bodies.
Work and study in la UA
6.1 Promote a balanced personal, family, and work life of UA members.
6.2 Detect, address and prevent sexual and gender harassment, and violence against women at the UA.
6.3 Include the gender approach in our occupational safety and health policy.
Training
7.1 Train the university community with resources and tools for the inclusion of a gender perspective in the teaching and research activity and in administrative management
Gender equality in sports and physical activity
8.1. Raise awareness to the university community about the lack of effective equality between men and women in sports, and the existence of gender stereotypes associated to their practice.
8.2. Recognize women's participation in sports and promote their practice amongst women.
8.3. Gender main streaming in the undergraduate degree in Physical Activity and Sports Sciences.
8.4. Promote research on the women’s role in sports and physical activity and disseminate the results.
8.5. Promote the inclusion of women in those areas of sports management and administration in which they are underrepresented.
8.6. Detect, address and prevent sexual harassment, harassment based on sexual orientation and gender violence in UA sports activities.
8.7. Gender main streaming in the planning and management of the sports activities offered by the UA.

Credit recognition and transfer
The University of Alicante Regulations on Credit Recognition and Transfer in Official Studies is currently in the process of adaptation to Royal Decree 822/2021 of 28 September.
Credit Recognition
Recognition is understood as the acceptance of credits -by a university- which have been obtained in official courses, in the same university or a different one and counted for the purpose of obtaining an official degree. Likewise, credits taken in other official higher education courses or in university courses leading to the awarding of other degrees, in accordance with the provisions of the
Organic Law 2/2023 of March 22, of the University System.
Accredited work and professional experience may also be recognised in the form of credits that will count towards the award of an official qualification, provided that this experience is related to the content of this undergraduate degree.
Credit Transfer
Credit transfer consists of including in the student’s transcript credits obtained in previous official studies at either the UA or any other university, which did not result in the attainment of an official tittle qualification. The transfer of credits requires the prior admission of the student to the corresponding degree.
Modules, subjects or subjects transferred to the academic transcript of the new bachelor's degrees
will not be taken into account for the calculation of the grade point average.
In the case of simultaneous studies, the credits therefor achieved will not be transferred, unless they are eligible for recognition or the student renounces to such simultaneity due to the student’s withdrawal from the studies.
CREDIT RECOGNITION FOR UNDERGRADUATE, MASTER'S AND PhD DEGREES
Recognition of credits obtained in official studies according to previous university regulations:
In the case of credits obtained in UA
official degrees
regulated by the Royal Decree 1497/1987, the recognition is achieved by taking into account the table for credit adjustment of those degrees regulated by Royal Decree 1393/2007 and the Royal Decree 861/2010 to be included in each report required to apply for the verification of the University of Alicante degrees.
In the case of credits obtained from
other official degrees
, they can be recognised by adapting the knowledge associated to subjects or courses taken by the student and those included in the study programmes meeting the following criteria:
that the number of credits, or where appropriate, hours, is at least 75% of the number of credits or hours of the subjects for which credit recognition is requested, and
obtain, at least, 75% of the content of the subjects for which credit recognition is sought.
Recognition of the credits obtained from the study programmes in force and non-official degrees:
Credits can be recognised taking into account the adjustment of the skills and knowledge acquired by the student from other modules, subjects and courses and from those included in the study programmes or cross-curricular courses that meet the following criteria:
The number of credits is at least 75% of the number of credits of the subjects for which credit recognition is requested.
It contains, at least, 75% of the content of the subjects for which credit recognition is requested.
It confers at least 75% of the skills of the subjects for which credit recognition is requested.
As for undergraduate degrees, the recognition of credits must also meet the following criteria:
If the undergraduate degree to which the student intends to gain access belongs to the same branch of knowledge, the credits corresponding to core subjects of that branch are eligible for recognition, with the original name, credits and mark.
If the undergraduate degree to which the student intends to gain access belongs to a branch of knowledge other than the original, the credits corresponding to core subjects of that new branch are also eligible for recognition.
In any case, the credits corresponding to the final-year undergraduate project and the Master's final project cannot be recognised.
Recognition of previous work and/or professional experience:
Accredited work and professional experience can be also recognised with credits that will count towards obtaining an official degree, provided that they confer at least 75% of the competences of the subjects eligible for credit recognition.
To be taken into account:
The number of credits allocated to
work or professional experience and to non-official university studies
cannot exceed, as a whole, 15 % of the total credits making up the curriculum. The recognition of those credits cannot be counted for the purpose of the student’s point-system assessment.
Notwithstanding the above,
credits from UA-only degrees
may, exceptionally, be subject to recognition in a higher percentage or, where appropriate, be subject to recognition in their entirety provided that the corresponding UAdegree has been phased out and replaced by an official degree.
Recognition of credits in mobility programmes:
Students who participate in national or international mobility programmes signed by the University of Alicante, while studying in other higher education institutions, will obtain full recognition by means of a learning agreement established before their departure. Likewise, credits completed through official courses regulated by inter-university agreements will be recognised if they specifically state so. In both cases, a report from the Centre or University Commissions will not be necessary, as such recognition is subject to a prior agreement.
Recognition of Higher Level Training Cycle subjects:
Students with a Higher Level Training Cycle may apply for the recognition of subjects in the Degree as long as they belong to the same branch of knowledge. Students applying for recognition from a training cycle need only present the academic certificate issued by the vocational training centre.
There are recognition tables from the different training cycles to UA degrees. More information at the corresponding centres.

General objectives
The general objective of the new degree in Biomedical Engineering is to prepare professionals with a broad and solid training that prepares them to manage and carry out tasks in all phases of the life cycle of systems, applications and products that solve problems related to Information and Communication Technologies in the field of health, applying their scientific knowledge and the methods and techniques of engineering. In general terms, Graduates in Biomedical Engineering are able to learn to know, do, live and be, in their personal, professional and social spheres, in accordance with the UNESCO report on the prospects for education in the 21st century.
top
Structure
The Bachelor's Degree in Biomedical Engineering is organised into semester subjects of 6 European ECTS credits each. Specifically, students must take 30 credits in each semester, thus reaching 60 credits per academic year and a total of 240 credits in four academic years.
In order to facilitate the possibility of combining studies with other activities, students may be part-time students, taking 30 ECTS credits per academic year.

Overview of the curriculum
The Graduate Degree in Biomedical Engineering from the University of Alicante is an official university degree, and its syllabus will have a duration of 240 European credits.
The 60-credit basic training block (6 of which are complementary and correspond to the biology subject belonging to the Health Sciences area), the 156-credit block of compulsory subjects (not counting the 12 credits corresponding to the Undergrate Thesis) and the credits of optional subjects, including external internships and English, must be taken.
Students are guaranteed the possibility of obtaining academic recognition of up to a maximum of 12 optional credits from the syllabus for participation in university cultural, sporting, student representation, solidarity and cooperation activities.
top
Distinctive Features
One of the most representative features of the degree in Biomedical Engineering at the University of Alicante is its
multidisciplinary training
in the areas of engineering and health. It is a degree coordinated by the Higher Polytechnic School in which the faculties of Science and Health Sciences also participate. Biomedical engineering studies incorporate a common base, typical of the branch of engineering and architecture, with mathematics and physics subjects and other subjects more specific to the degree such as chemistry and biology; these basic contents are complemented in higher courses with other subjects applied to the field of biomedical engineering, such as electronics, programming, instrumentation, telemedicine or biomechanics. To complete the training in biomedical engineering, health-related subjects such as anatomy, physiology and physiopathology are also taught. The training provided means that graduates have a great capacity for abstraction and the ability to propose solutions from an engineering point of view in a wide range of fields.
The results of biomedical engineering projects have a great social impact due to the field to which they are applied, health care in all its aspects. In this sense, at the University of Alicante there are
research
groups whose activity contributes to maintaining the biomedical engineering studies at the University of Alicante at the highest academic level, providing
innovation
and current relevance in the contents taught. Furthermore, the participation in the studies of the Higher Polytechnic School, the Faculty of Health Sciences and the Faculty of Sciences facilitates interdisciplinarity and the proposal of applied research projects in which groups from different areas of specialisation participate. The Higher Polytechnic School, through the teaching staff of the biomedical engineering degree, participates at national level in the Spanish Society of Biomedical Engineering and, at international level, in the Engineering in Medicine and Biology Society (EMBS) of the IEEE.
The academic nature of the different subjects varies according to the subject and its training objectives. In general, the Polytechnic School is aware of the importance of
practical training
and this is reflected in the various practical training resources such as:
Seminars and/or practical problems:
Activities involving the application of theoretical content, the development of complementary aspects of the subject, the resolution of questions and problems that the teacher proposes to the students in advance and which aim to encourage the active and critical participation of the student.
Practical computer classes:
These are sessions in which computer systems are used to develop projects in the field of biomedical engineering or to study applications that are used professionally in this field..
Practical lab classes:
These are sessions in which experiments, calculations or data processing and analysis procedures are planned and carried out, and the results obtained are interpreted.
In addition, depending on the nature of the subjects, the following training activities, among others, are carried out: individual or group work (planning, execution, presentation and debate), attendance at conferences, meetings or scientific discussions, visits to companies and institutions, etc. In theory and problem-solving classes, the active participation of students is encouraged. In these classes, the teaching staff use technological resources, including interactive presentations in the classroom, virtual tutorials and another series of utilities through the intranet of the University of Alicante (UACloud) that allow, for example, the realization of non-face-to-face activities of autonomous learning and its monitoring by the teaching staff (self-assessment, debates, forums, etc...). The use of the UACloud is widespread in all subjects and allows students to access all information (teaching guide) and all related materials (presentations, questions, supplementary material, etc.) from anywhere.
It is important to highlight the possibility of carrying out curricular academic internships in companies, i.e. as part of the syllabus, through the various agreements that the University of Alicante has signed with technology companies in the field of biomedical engineering and clinical centres and hospitals, both public and private. It is also worth mentioning the Undergrate Thesis, many of which are multidisciplinary in nature and directed by two tutors, one from the engineering area and the other from the health field; these projects are the culmination of the students' studies and demonstrate all the skills acquired in the degree.

Career Opportunities
The importance of health-related professional activity is inherent to the field of application: the survival instinct of individuals raises the importance of health care and all related tasks to a maximum degree. On the other hand, the development throughout human history of health care activities has led to the integration of health aspects with a multitude of professional sectors, from the industrial sector (e.g. related to the manufacture of medical devices) to the public administration sector (e.g. related to public health).
It is important to note that the development of health care over the centuries has made it a discipline with special characteristics (e.g. strong standardisation) that require professionals in this field to have multidisciplinary knowledge covering both the health sector itself and the other sectors within it.
Below is a list of sectors associated with the field of health in which graduates in Biomedical Engineering for Health could develop their professional activity.
Medical device industry sector
In this sector, Biomedical Engineering graduates could participate in interdisciplinary work teams dedicated to the development and/or maintenance of medical equipment for diagnosis, treatment and/or monitoring of patients, including all related software. The basic multidisciplinary knowledge of these graduates makes them excellent interlocutors between medical teams and industrial design and development teams.
Also associated with the industrial sector, they can provide consultancy, quality control and maintenance management of equipment within clinical engineering or electro-medical departments, as part of after-sales services or maintenance contracts.
The high level of standardisation in the health sector has been highlighted, especially when it comes to devices that can affect people's health. Such professionals can also participate in international standardisation committees and bodies as well as in certification agencies for medical devices.
Healthcare sector
The evolution of health care, especially since the appearance of information technologies, makes it necessary for professionals with technological knowledge to become indirectly involved in health care. Thus, for example, graduates in Biomedical Engineering are qualified to participate in the implementation and administration of networks, infrastructures and information systems in hospital departments and clinics, facilitating the clinical activity of health professionals.
The multidisciplinary knowledge that graduates have obtained in their Biomedical Engineering studies gives them an interdisciplinary vision, ideal for acting as health technology consultants and/or consultants in health information systems.
Social organisations sector
Social organisations are an indispensable sector in society, complementary to the health care sector and continuously developing. Engineers for health can work professionally in activities similar to those they can perform in the health care sector, having the basis to become excellent consultants on patient support systems and health workers. They can also advise on technological issues and support the activities of health societies.
Public administration sector
Public administration is another sector in which these professionals can actively develop their work, as health care is organised directly by the public administration through bodies at various levels: national, regional and local. Health engineers can participate in this organisation at all levels, advising on the definition of strategic policies related to the coordination of technology and innovation in public and subsidised health centres.
The technology and health knowledge of these professionals enables them to participate in the committees for the adaptation and integration of standards that are established in the field of health within the public administration.
Another of the fundamental tasks in public administration related to the field of health is the monitoring and control of public health. Graduates in Biomedical Engineering know the necessary tools to participate from a technological point of view in the analysis of clinical information carried out in public health studies.
Information and communications technology sector
In recent years, the ICT sector has made great progress in the field of healthcare, resulting in a clear integration between clinical activity and information systems. Healthcare engineers can participate in the development, maintenance and implementation of clinical information systems.
The multidisciplinary knowledge acquired during their degree studies enables these professionals to support both health specialists and other ICT professionals in their tasks with the aim of improving their efficiency. They also become suitable professionals to participate in the organisation and supervision of multidisciplinary health-ICT teams.
Pharmaceutical sector
The pharmaceutical sector is another of the sectors related to health care that makes intensive use of technological knowledge to advance the development of its products. The skills acquired in Biomedical Engineering studies enable graduates to join technological development teams for companies involved in the development and distribution of pharmaceuticals. Biomedical Engineering professionals can carry out their tasks as qualified technicians in companies related to the management and computer analysis of biological data.
Health research sector
Finally, research in the field of health is in continuous development with the aim of optimising diagnostic techniques and treatments to improve people's health, reduce costs, etc. Graduates in Biomedical Engineering have the necessary knowledge base to successfully participate in multidisciplinary research tasks related to the evaluation and development of clinical information systems, the exploitation of clinical data, the integration of technologies in clinical activity, etc.
Professions for which it trains:
health technology consultant, clinical systems analyst, designer and developer of medical applications, health data analyst, technology manager in health areas, telemedicine systems designer, developer of mobile health applications, technology coordinator of multidisciplinary teams in health, advisor in health systems integration, medical information systems analyst, coordinator of technological innovation in health centres, technology advisor in public health, computational analyst of biological data, researcher in technology applied to health. In general, all those professions in the field of health care that involve the use or need for information and communication technologies.

Página de inicio del sitio
La creación de la Universidad de Alicante data de 1979. A pesar de su relativa juventud, es una Institución reconocida no ya solo en el ámbito local o nacional, sino también global. No somos ajenos a la realidad social, económica, tecnológica y cultural que se encuentra en constante cambio. Es por eso que la Universidad Alicante está comprometida con el resto de Instituciones y Entidades, de carácter público o privado, nacional o internacional, que interactúan con nosotros o puedan hacerlo en un futuro no lejano.
Por lo anterior, nace esta Dirección de Relaciones y Proyectos Institucionales con un doble objetivo: de un lado impulsar y desarrollar proyectos institucionales con el apoyo del Servicio OGPI, y de otro mantener y fortalecer las relaciones con todos los agentes externos que se relacionan con la Universidad de Alicante.
Para el primer objetivo nos apoyamos en el Servicio OGPI (Oficina de Gestión de Proyectos Institucionales), y para el segundo en la Unidad de Convenios, (en lo relativo a la administración de Convenios y de las Cátedras Institucionales).
Juan Llopis Taverner
Director de Relaciones y Proyectos Institucionales

Tutorial Action Plan (TAP)
The University of Alicante's Tutorial Action Programme (TAP) aims to provide university students with support and advice at the beginning and throughout their university studies. The tutorial action provided by the TAP responds to two priority needs of university students: on the one hand, their welcome and inclusion in the university context; and, on the other hand, support and guidance in the development of their academic and professional itinerary.
TAP is considered as an educational action of guidance and support for students, offered by the University of Alicante in all its centres. It aims to improve the quality of education by offering a service of accompaniment, advice and tutoring to all students at the University of Alicante.
Within tutorial action, three key TAP implementation processes stand out:
At the time of admission to the University, reception and adaptation.
Academic and personal counselling and tutoring during the years of residence.
At the end of the studies, counselling and tutoring focused on the academic and/or professional future.
The TAP structure of each centre has a coordinator and a specific tutorial team. You can access the specific information of the TAP of your Faculty or School in the following link:

Internships in companies and institutions
Practices will allow students to expand their training through the application of the theoretical knowledge acquired in the classroom in the business world. This first making contact with the world of work can be very useful when students get their degree. Another factor to consider is that be recognized the student practiced in elective credits or elective hours reflecting on his record with the subject "internship".
The internship studies are structured as one subject (see registration). Each credit ECTS of this subjert is equivalent to 25 hours of the internship.
The schedule will be the stipulated between the company and the student, always respecting the right of students to attend the examinations.
Students will be subject to the duration, calendar, schedule, place, activity and other conditions laid down in the corresponding agreement with the company or institution in which the practice is carried out.
Practices does not imply any employment relationship with the company, so it is not obliged to remunerate the work done by the student, although the company can provide, if desired, an economic student bag.

Undergraduate Thesis
All the official undergraduate degrees must be completed by preparing and defending a final year project, which must be done in the final phase of the studies and be aimed at the assessment of competences associated to the degree.
The final year project must be an original, independent and personal work. The elaboration shall be individual. Each student will prepare this project under the supervision of a tutor, allowing students to show the received training content in an integrated many, as well as the acquired competences associated to the undergraduate degree.
In order to
register in the final year project
, students must comply with the requirements established in the "Regulations for continuation studies for students registered in undergraduate degrees at the University of Alicante". Among the requirements established to be able to register in the final year project, a minimum of 168 credits must be passed in undergraduate degrees with a total of 240 credits, and a minimum of 210 credits in undergraduate degrees with a total of 300 credits or more.
In order for
the final year project to be assessed
, a B1 level of a foreign language (B2 is recommended) must be confirmed.

Language teaching competence
So far, those graduates who wished to begin a career in non-university teaching had to obtain the
Teaching Competence Certificate
(Valencian and / or foreign languages). With the new legislation of the Generalitat, this way disappears.
In the new regulations published by the Generalitat on multilingualism (
Law 4/2018
) and the linguistic competence necessary for access to and exercise of the teaching function in the Valencian educational system (
Order 3/2020
), modified by
Order 4/2021
, the consideration of this certificate has changed, and it will disappear from now on. Whoever is following an itinerary to obtain the Teaching CompetenceCertificate must bear in mind that a deadline is
established until October 29, 2021
to be able to register it. If you obtain the degree and the itinerary before that date, you will be able to register your certificate. After it, the registration will be closed.
The requirements for access to public service are set by the specific call for each public examination contest. However, taking the
2020 call
as a reference and considering that the Teaching CompetenceCertificate in Valencian is no longer the linguistic requirement, the next calls for public examination contests will most likely establish the requirement to accredit the
C1 level of knowledge of Spanish and C1 for Valencian
(the
Mitjà
certificate is completely equivalent). Teachers who have studied in Spain will be exempt from accrediting the C1 in Spanish.
The
C1 level certificate
of knowledge of Valencian from the UA, the JQCV or equivalent (such as the certificates of the CIEACOVA and the EOI) will be the necessary language competence to teach non-linguistic subjects in Early Childhood Education in Valencian, Primary Education, Special Education, Compulsory Secondary Education, Baccalaureate, Vocational Training Cycles and Adult Training.
As of the academic year 2026-2027
, the language proficiency necessary for teaching non-linguistic subjects in a foreign language will be
the C1 level certificate for that language
.
Until the academic year 2026-2027
, the
B2 level of English
must be accredited.
The Teaching CompetenceCertificate in Valencian will be considered a merit in the public examinations’ contests for the teaching bodies. This certificate can be used as proof of the linguistic requirement,
being equivalent to C1
, for public examination contests, for transfer contests and special service appointments, and therefore enables to teach in Valencian under the same conditions as a C1.
If used as accreditation of the linguistic requirement, the certificate will not count as a merit.

Credit recognition and transfer
Reconocimiento de Créditos es la aceptación por una universidad de los créditos que, habiendo sido obtenidos en otras enseñanzas oficiales, en la misma u otra universidad, son computados en otras distintas a efectos de la obtención de un título oficial. Es decir, es la aceptación de asignaturas superadas en unos estudios universitarios cuando el estudiante accede a una nueva titulación. El concepto de "reconocimiento" ha sustituido, en los estudios de grado, al antiguo concepto de convalidación/adaptación.
Transferencia de créditos es la inclusión en el expediente y en los documentos académicos oficiales acreditativos de las enseñanzas seguidas por cada estudiante de la totalidad de los créditos obtenidos en enseñanzas oficiales cursadas con anterioridad en la misma o en otra universidad, que no hayan conducido a la obtención de un título oficial.

The Governing Council of the University of Alicante has regulated the procedures and
criteria for the evaluation and grading of results
, the scheduling and convocation of exams,
the publication of grades
and, where appropriate, the procedures for
reviewing and claiming grades
, as well as the completion and modification of reports.
This regulation adapts the criteria of the teaching programme to the continuous assessment system that inspired the implementation of the current official degrees of the UA. It also brings together and updates previous regulations.
The main measures are:
Continuous assessment
, which will be the general assessment system.
The establishment of alternative assessment systems for students entitled to
"curricular adaptation"
is envisaged.
The evaluation
criteria
will be
public and objective
, will be included before the enrolment periods in the "Teaching Guides" of the subjects and cannot be modified during the academic year except for justified reasons.
There will be
recoverable and non-recoverable assessment activities
that will appear in the "Teaching Guides" of the subjects.
Two assessment periods
are established:
ordinary and extraordinary
, which will appear in the annual
academic calendar
.
The calendar of final
exams may not be modified, except in cases of force majeure. The date of the final exams of each subject will be known before the enrolment periods, at least 30 days in advance.
The grading system will be numerical from 0 to 10 to one decimal place
. "Honours" may be awarded to students with a grade equal to or higher than 9, with a maximum of 5% of students enrolled in a subject or 1 for subjects with 20 or fewer students.
The
results
of partial and final tests will be
published via
UACloud
. For the former, no later than 30 calendar days. For the latter, within the period established in the academic calendar. In addition, for final exams, the place, date and time for revision will be indicated at least 48 hours in advance.
The procedure for the
review and appeal
of grades is established.

What is "curriculum adaptation" and who can request it?
"Curriculum adaptation" is a series of academic and technical actions which can be carried out in order to facilitate students' access, stay and maximum progress during the academic year.
Applications for curriculum adaptation will be processed according to confidentiality and privacy principles.
Students who can apply for curriculum adaptation are divided in two groups:
First group
. Students proving a 33% physical, sensory, or mental
disability
or higher, and students with specific needs for
educational support (SNES)
, congenital or not.
Second group
. Students proving that they are elite, high-level, or university
athletes
;
gender violence
victims; new parents
, students undergoing a child adoption or custody
situation; students with
dependent children under 3 years old
, or taking care for dependent family members; students whose
work hours are in conflict with
class hours; members of the University of Alicante governing bodies, participants in trophies, cultural and academic activities on behalf of the UA, and students engaged in mobility programmes.
Applications for curriculum adaptation will be submitted two weeks before the beginning of the academic year or term. Applications submitted outside these two periods due to unforeseen circumstances will be exceptionally admitted.
Applications from students in the first group will be submitted to the Student Support Centre, and the ones from the second group will go to the corresponding centre (
faculties/polytechnic school).
There are two levels of curriculum adaptation:
In-class support
may consist on adaptation of materials, lighting or sound quality in the classrooms. Adaptation of practical sessions and academic contents, and even use of alternative or complementary communication systems is available. Moreover, justified absences will be taken into account (maximum 25% per subject)
Adaptation of assessment tests
may consist in extended time for tests or transcription of tests to other alternative communication systems.

UA researcher Javier García wins the Juan de la Cierva National Research Award
The National Research Awards ceremony was held at the Borja ducal palace in Gandía, presided over by the King and Queen of Spain
García has defended the truth of science in the face of the problems that society must face
Gandia, Thursday 14 March 2024
University of Alicante Professor of
Inorganic Chemistry
Javier García Martínez, received the  'Juan de la Cierva' National Research Award in the area of Technology Transfer from the King and Queen of Spain today at a ceremony held at the Ducal Palace of the Borja family in Gandía.
The ceremony was held at 12 noon, hosted by journalist Lara Siscar and, after a few words from Minister of Science, Innovation and Universities Diana Morant, the videos of the award winners were shown and the King and Queen of Spain presented the awards. The ceremony concluded with a panel discussion with the participation of representatives of the 2023 National Research Awards that were presented today, including UA professor Javier García.
Javier García, a researcher at the University of Alicante, said that receiving the National Research Award is—above all—an important recognition of the work they have been doing in the UA Molecular Nanotechnology Laboratory. Here, they have developed new chemical processes that allow them to develop and transform waste into products with high added value, convert plastics into clean fuels and manufacture medicines with a more environmentally friendly alternative.
García stressed that the research that has earned him the high distinction has served to develop and transfer to the market a new family of catalysts that are used today around the world to reduce CO2 emissions and which are being marketed by different multinationals.
The UA researcher also explained that these National Research Awards are of significant relevance to recognise the best cutting-edge research in Spain. This message is key because it will undoubtedly help us create new scientific vocations in our country. According to the researcher, Science is not an accessory, it is not a luxury, it is a priority, it is at the basis of the new companies that are redefining our future, whether they are based on AI, nanotechnology or gene editing.
Finally, the new National Research Award winner added that the problems we face can only be overcome with more and better science. In a time when economic, social and geopolitical conditions are uncertain, we need the certainty and solutions provided by science and to make decisions based on evidence.
The award ceremony was also attended by University of Alicante Vice President for
Research
Juan Mora, who wanted to express his sincere congratulations to Professor García Martínez for this award, the result of his obvious talent and research effort that has led him to become an international benchmark in the field of nanomaterials chemistry.
Mora said that the University of Alicante is proud to have a new researcher awarded with the National Research Award, the highest recognition for science granted by the Spanish Government. The award, the second in a row received by a researcher from the University of Alicante, is a recognition of the excellent research activity carried out on our campus and is a boost to research and knowledge transfer at our university.
Additionally, the King closed the ceremony highlighting the role of science and scientists in strengthening our country through their excellent work and stressed that all their work and dedication deserve our gratitude and esteem. Felipe VI pointed out that the people we are recognising today are necessary and a source of pride for a country that has ambitions for the future. A future that is intimately linked to scientific research in order to achieve the best possible future for generations to come.
His Majesty ended his speech by paraphrasing Vicent Andrés Estellés "science is the key that opens all doors”, he said and reminded us that it is necessary to reiterate that investing in science is investing in the future”.
Minister of Science, Innovation and Universities Diana Morant referred in her speech to the award winners as "the architects of our future" and pointed out that "strengthening research is the key to strengthening all citizens".

La cerimònia de lliurament dels Premis Nacionals d’Investigació s’ha celebrat al Palau Ducal dels Borja a Gandia presidida pels Reis d’Espanya
García ha reivindicat ‘la certesa de la ciència’ davant dels problemes que ha d’enfrontar la societat
Gandia. Dijous, 14 de març de 2024
El catedràtic de
Química Inorgànica
de la Universitat d’Alacant, Javier García Martínez, ha rebut avui, de mans dels Reis d’Espanya, el Premi Nacional d’Investigació «Juan de la Cierva», en l’àrea de Transferència de Tecnologia en una cerimònia que ha tingut lloc al Palau Ducal dels Borja a Gandia.
L’acte s’ha celebrat a les 12 hores conduït per la periodista Lara Siscar i, després d’unes paraules de la ministra de Ciència, Innovació i Universitats, Diana Morant, s’han projectat els vídeos dels premiats i els Reis han fet lliurament dels guardons. La cerimònia ha conclòs amb una taula redona en la qual han intervingut representants dels Premis Nacionals d’Investigació 2023 que avui s’han lliurat, entre els quals es trobava el catedràtic de la UA, Javier García.
Javier García, investigador de la Universitat d’Alacant ha assenyalat que «rebre el Premi Nacional d’Investigació suposa sobretot un reconeixement molt important al treball que fem en el Laboratori de Nanotecnologia Molecular de la UA. Ací hem desenvolupat nous processos químics que ens permeten desenvolupar i transformar residus en productes d’alt valor afegit, convertir plàstics en combustibles nets i fabricar medicaments d’una forma molt més sostenible».
García ha recalcat que la investigació que li ha merescut l’alta distinció «ha servit per a desenvolupar i transferir al mercat una nova família de catalitzadors que s’utilitzen avui a tot el món per a reduir les emissions de CO₂ i que estan comercialitzant diferents multinacionals».
L’investigador de la UA també ha assegurat que «aquests Premis Nacionals d’Investigació són molt rellevants ja que és clau reconèixer la millor investigació que es fa al nostre país. I és que és possible fer investigació d’avantguarda a Espanya. Aquest missatge és clau perquè, sens dubte, ajudarà a crear noves vocacions científiques al nostre país. La ciència no és una cosa accessòria, no és un luxe, és una cosa prioritària, està en la base de les noves empreses que estan redefinint el nostre futur, bé siguen les que estan basades en IA, en nanotecnologia o en edició genètica».
Finalment, el nou Premi Nacional d’Investigació ha postil·lat que «els problemes a què ens enfrontem només podran ser superats amb més ciència i millor. En un temps caracteritzat per la incertesa, econòmica, social i geopolítica, necessitem la certesa i les solucions que ens aporta la ciència i prendre decisions basades en evidències».
A la cerimònia de lliurament dels premis també ha assistit el vicerector d’Investigació de la Universitat d’Alacant, Juan Mora, qui ha volgut «expressar la meua més sincera enhorabona al professor García Martínez per aquest premi fruit del seu indubtable talent i esforç investigador que l’ha fet convertir-se en un referent internacional en l’àmbit de la química dels nanomaterials».
Mora ha assegurat que «per a la Universitat d’Alacant és un orgull comptar amb un nou investigador guardonat amb el Premi Nacional d’Investigació, el més alt reconeixement a la ciència atorgat pel Govern d’Espanya. El Premi, segon consecutiu rebut per un investigador de la Universitat d’Alacant, és un reconeixement a l’excel·lència de l’activitat investigadora duta a terme al nostre campus i suposa un impuls a la investigació i a la transferència de coneixement en la nostra universitat».
Per part seua, el rei, encarregat de tancar l’acte, ha reivindicat el paper de la ciència i dels científics per a «l’enfortiment del nostre país a través de la seua excel·lent labor» i ha recalcat que tot «el seu treball i dedicació mereixen la nostra gratitud i estima». Felip VI ha assenyalat que les «persones que avui reconeixem són necessàries i són un orgull per a un país que té ambició de futur. Un futur que està íntimament lligat a la investigació científica per a aconseguir el millor futur possible i arribar a les generacions futures».
Sa majestat ha finalitzat la intervenció parafrasejant Vicent Andrés Estellés «la ciència és la clau que obri totes les portes», ha afirmat i ha recordat que «és necessari reiterar que invertir en ciència és invertir en futur».
La ministra de de Ciència, Innovació i Universitats, Diana Morant, s’ha referit en la seua intervenció als premiats com «els arquitectes del nostre futur» i ha ressenyat que «enfortir la investigació és la clau per a enfortir a tota la ciutadania».

Vicerrectorado de Investigación
Juan Mora
Vicerrector de Investigación
Agenda institucional del Vicerrector
La Universidad de Alicante ha experimentado en los últimos años un notable avance en su proyección nacional e internacional gracias al sustancial incremento en los resultados obtenidos por nuestros Grupos de Investigación. Mi compromiso como Vicerrector de Investigación es el de seguir avanzando en esta línea, fomentando la investigación en todos los campos científicos y tecnológicos. Para ello es fundamental facilitar y estimular la actividad investigadora del personal docente e investigador en entornos saludables y paritarios, apostar por la retención y captación de joven talento investigador y fomentar la cooperación científica y la internacionalización de nuestros grupos de investigación.
Para alcanzar este enorme reto, me valgo de mi experiencia profesional y de la que he adquirido durante los últimos años al frente del Secretariado de Investigación. También cuento con la de las Directoras de Secretariado y de la Escuela Internacional de Doctorado que me acompañan en este proyecto, así como de un equipo humano de reconocida experiencia y prestigio: todos los técnicos y gestores de los diferentes Servicios y Unidades que forman parte esencial de este vicerrectorado.
Animo a toda la Comunidad Universitaria a compartir con nosotros este objetivo y me pongo a vuestra disposición para que, entre todos, consigamos que la Universidad de Alicante se convierta en un referente de excelencia internacional en materia de investigación.

campus
CampusGigabytGirl has been designed within the framework of an action to
carry out awareness campaigns aimed at secondary school female students focused on fighting fighting gender stereotypes when it comes to choosing university studies
included in the University of Alicante's 2nd Plan for Equal Opportunities for Women and Men.
Turning the world of computers and information technologies into a forward-looking project for women is the goal of GigabytGirl, a pioneer experience inspired by similar campaigns carried out by technology giants like Google or Microsoft and now carried out in Spanish universities that was launched in the week between 15 to 19 June where a total of 20 secondary school female students participate in this event organised by the Polytechnic School and the Gender Equality Unit.
This first action includes a number of
recreational proposals
such as workshops in robotics and multimedia with modelling and computing animation and learning how to develop mobile applications using games.
